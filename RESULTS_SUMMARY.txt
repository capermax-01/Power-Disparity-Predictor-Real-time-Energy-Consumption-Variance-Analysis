## ðŸ’¡ INTERPRETATION GUIDE

### What is Power Disparity?
**Power Disparity** measures the variability/unpredictability of power consumption.

- **Formula**: Coefficient of Variation (CV) = (Standard Deviation / Mean) Ã— 100%

### Disparity Levels
- **CV > 100%**: HIGH - Very unpredictable, hard to manage
- **CV 50-100%**: MEDIUM - Moderate variability, needs planning
- **CV < 50%**: LOW - Predictable, good for baseline planning
- **CV â‰ˆ 0%**: STABLE - Constant consumption, ideal for scheduling

### Why It Matters
1. **Grid Management**: Predicting variability improves load balancing
2. **Equipment Planning**: High-disparity devices need redundancy
3. **Cost Optimization**: Schedule flexible loads during low-disparity periods
4. **Maintenance**: Monitor high-disparity appliances for degradation
5. **Capacity Planning**: Design systems with variability in mind
